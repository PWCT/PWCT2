Analysis




How do we proceed that?



Lets look closer at the number: 1234567, we can break it down into groups of three.
The number “1” refers to the group Million.
The number “234” is for the group of Thousands.
And in the last group, the number “567” has no name for its group.
But like the previous groups, it is composed of different elements, the elements (or items) are:

5 hundred 6 tens 7 ones

So hundreds, tens and the ones.

The logic is obvious.
We need to divide the entire input (1,234,567) into groups,
Assign them the correct group name (Million, Thousand ... etc.) and then break them down (groups of three) into their elements (Hundreds, Tens and Ones).
We start at the end (from right to left ... because of the small numbers) and then output everything in reverse order.

It is important to first look at the structure of the number. eg: 1234567

hto
|||
1 234 567
| |
g g

o: ones ( 1-19)
t: tens (20-90)
h: "Hundred"
g: Groups (Thousand, Hundred, Tens ...)

Let's start from right to left ( <-- ).
7, 6 and 5 consists of: seven (Ones) and sixty (Tens) and five (Hundreds).
This is the first group (seen from left to right à ) that is to be evaluated and converted.

We want to call the numbers 1..19 the Ones.
The whole numbers 20..90 the Tens.
With that, every number from 1 to 999 can be converted into words.
First of all the last two digits - 67 (sixty seven) are in the same group (Ones) as the first digit 5 (five "Hundred").

The digits in the group when combined left to right ( --> )show up as: five hundred sixty seven.

So once again, in order:

1 million
234 thousand
567 (no group name)

Each of the groups can consist of one, two or three numbers. This is repeated.
We'll look at the last three groups: six hundred seventy-eight.
The first number, if there is one, is always 1-9 (Hundred).
Then there is a group of two, 78, and the first number belongs to the Tens and the last (third) to the Ones.
Now the task is practically solved.

So we need a procedure that searches the input for groups of 3, assigns them a corresponding name (Million, Thousand, etc.),
then breaks down the individual groups of 3, determines the Hundreds and Tens and assembles them with Ones.

All right? Now we can proceed further.
